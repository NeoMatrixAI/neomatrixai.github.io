the idea

It works like a mixing board.

Each strategy is a channel. They all get mixed down into one real set of trades — the only thing the exchange sees. But every channel keeps its own meter, so you always know how each strategy is doing on its own.

what's changing

You won't be limited by accounts anymore.

Exchanges only give you a few accounts, and today each strategy needs its own. So you run out of accounts long before you run out of ideas. The compositor puts many strategies into a single account — so you can run as many as you like.

how it's organized

Inside that account: groups, and strategies.

There are three levels, and it's simple. Your one real account holds several groups. Each group holds as many strategies as you want. Groups are just a way to organize — say, a calm group and an aggressive one — and you get a scorecard for each group too, not only for each strategy.

In one line: 1 account → many groups → many strategies in each. (If you're technical: the account is a fund, a group is a portfolio, a strategy is an alpha — but the names don't matter here.)

how it works

Every minute, three things happen.

1

Each active strategy decides what it wants to trade right now.

2

The compositor mixes them all into a single order.

3

That one order goes to your account — the same way it does today.

Meanwhile, each strategy's own scorecard updates — minute by minute, just like your account does now. And there are safety checks built in: it never over-invests, it never force-sells a strategy that hit an error, and an empty signal never wipes your account.

the honest part

Can you trust each strategy's numbers?

Yes — and here's why. Since strategies share one account, their trades combine. If one buys a coin and another sells the same coin, the account only holds the difference:

what really trades

The account only trades the net — but both strategies are still scored in full, so you always know which one is actually working. Then every minute we add all the scorecards back up and compare them to your real account. If there's any small difference — from fees or timing — we show it. We never hide it.

the plan

It arrives in steps.

Here's the order we're building it in — each step adds something you can actually use.

1

Trade on more than one exchange — Bitget and Binance to start — tested end to end before it goes live.

The heart of it — mixing many strategies into one order, with the safety checks. If you only run one strategy, nothing about it changes.

5

The system can shift money between strategies by your rules — all on paper, with no surprise trades.

A careful move to the new version with no gap in your history — rehearsed first, then switched on.
